STREET PERFORMER COACH

A new space for reflection and performance planning


GOAL: Create a product for street performers through user research and a collaborative design process

USER RESEARCH: Contextual Inquiry and Semi-structured Interviewing

We searched for street performers and buskers in the Boston area. We watched them perform, observed the way they set up and tore down before and after a performance, and met with them in coffee shops to ask them in depth about their experiences with street performing.

PERSONAS:

The team came up with four personas to represent the people we interviewed.

Dreamer Dan has a strong artistic identity, but is underappreciated by his audience. He struggles to make ends meet but dreams of having a studio and live shows someday.
Pragmatic Peter is a successful musician who sees street performance as a means to promote his musical projects, and sells CDs and hands out flyers to interested audience members.
Jammin' Jana is a music student who street performs with her friends for fun. She loves the social aspect of collaborating with other musicians in the neighborhood.
Counter-Culture Cam is a rebellious self-taught musician and is more interested in self-expression than fitting in or achieving objective success. She street performs because it gives her more freedom than a retail job.

PRODUCT:

After ideating several ideas and co-designing with our users, we came up with the Street Performer Coach. The Street Performer Coach is a small device with a camera that the street performer can put on the ground next to their instrument case. The device records relevant data while the performer is playing, and the street performer can later review the data on their computer to reflect and plan for the future.



Once the device has recorded data for a few performances, it can begin giving recommendations to the street performer. The street performer can decide what days to perform based on what days and times have historically been the most successful.



There is also an earpiece that can be customized to fit the street performer's personality that will give them advice during their performance based on the data being captured - for example, it may suggest a song that fits the demographic of the audience, or tell them to change the tempo of their songs based on the mood and the weather.

The device is small so as not to distract the audience from the performance, and the earpiece fits neatly into the device when it is not in use, making it a simple addition to the street performer's routine without infringing upon their existing style.


ROLES AND REFLECTION:

I played a heavy role in the user research and co-design aspects of the project, and also owned the user interaction design for the interface the street performers would use to review the data presented on the computer.

I gained skills in conducting interviews and co-design sessions, and especially learned to put aside any of my personal assumptions. One large assumption I went into this project with was the idea that musicians perform on the streets because they can't get a "real job." By talking to our users, I discovered that most of them choose the busker lifestyle because they value the connections they make with their audience and the freedom of performance over a 9-to-5 job.
